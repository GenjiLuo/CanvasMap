A Compromise Aspect-Adaptive Cylindrical Projection

A new compromise cylindrical map projection family that adjusts the distribution of parallels to the aspect ratio of a canvas.

Developed by Bernhard Jenny (Oregon State University), Bojan Šavrič (Oregon State University), and Tom Patterson (US National Park Service).

Jenny, B., Šavrič, B. and Patterson, T. (—). A compromise aspect-adaptive cylindrical projection for world maps. International Journal of Geographical Information Science (in press).
